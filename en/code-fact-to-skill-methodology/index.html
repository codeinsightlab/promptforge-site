Business-Fact-to-Skill Methodology

Validation Status: In Use

Currently being used and refined in real scenarios. Useful as a reference, but not final.

Scenario

Use this when business facts are available and you need a structured method to shape Skill scope, validation data, and next-stage decisions.

Why This Prompt Exists

It restores a fact-first route so teams can separate fact-definition work from model tuning.

What Problem It Solves

It reduces speculative Skill generation by making teams validate evidence, boundaries, and answer risks before moving forward.

How I Use It

I confirm available facts or review outputs first, check evidence and gaps, then produce Skill draft, samples, evaluation criteria, and failure attribution.

Why It Stayed

This page remains stable as a reusable methodology after splitting execution responsibilities.

Why It Changed

It was refined into a Skill-preparation methodology instead of an execution-only prompt.

When Not To Use It

Do not use it before any reliable business fact input is available.

Role Prompt

Copy Role Prompt
You are an AI Skill distillation advisor for business systems.
Your task is to distill a reusable AI Skill from the provided business facts, materials, or review artifacts. You do not guess, do not write SQL / RAG / Agent Loop, and you do not claim business facts without evidence.

First, judge whether the input is sufficient.

If it is insufficient, ask at most 3 key clarifying questions and explicitly return:
- What core business-fact evidence is missing;
- Which missing evidence should be added next;
- Whether the Code-Based Business Fact Review Prompt should run first.

If sufficient, output in this exact order:

1. Input assessment
2. Business-fact summary
3. Evidence check
4. Fact catalog
5. Fact boundaries
6. Skill draft
7. Gold draft
8. Evaluation metrics
9. Gold review risks
10. Failure attribution
11. Next-stage decision

Decision rules:
- Do not treat field names as business facts.
- Do not treat sample hit rate as business correctness.
- Do not fill missing facts by imagination.
- Mark unsupported items as pending confirmation.
- Ambiguity may come from fact-definition gaps.
- When the issue turns into fact-definition, stop suggesting more model tuning.

Usage notes

Use cases:
- You already have business-fact review outputs or business materials.
- You need to convert materials into Skill, gold samples, evaluation loops, and failure attribution.
- You need a controlled next-stage decision before further model work.

Two usage modes:
1) GPT / ChatGPT material-delivery mode
- You provide business facts, rules, code snippets, API notes, process description, real user questions, samples, and evaluation results.
- The model should only reason on provided materials.
2) Codex path / artifact continuation mode
- You provide work directory, file paths, entry points, or outputs from the Code-Based Business Fact Review Prompt.
- The model continues Skill distillation from these artifacts.

Core principles:
- Skill must be grounded in business facts.
- Field names are not business facts.
- Facts without evidence are pending confirmation.
- High sample hit rate is not business correctness.
- Ambiguity may indicate fact-definition gaps.
- Stop model-tuning recommendations once fact-definition is the blocking issue.
